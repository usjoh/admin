Developer Agreement Active

The Residence at the ATHM — 491 Dutton Street, Lowell

Overview

The Developer Agreement between the Primary Board and developer John DeAngelis (Earth Realty) was executed on October 16, 2017. It established a 5-year base term with three optional 1-year extensions, each requiring a majority vote of the Primary Board.

All contractual extensions have been exhausted. The agreement's formal term ended April 3, 2026. The Primary Board subsequently granted a verbal extension to June 30, 2026 with six milestones but no bond, no penalties, and no intermediate deadlines.

Key implication: The current extension has no basis in the original agreement. All three contractual extension options have been used. The board's only enforcement mechanism is goodwill. Wally Smith and Anita Chmilarski are monitoring informally.

The developer currently owns only Unit 207. Once that unit is sold, the Developer Agreement auto-terminates. The developer owes $42,935.50 to the association ($26,200 to Primary + $16,735.50 to Museum). Unit 207 closing paperwork is in progress.

Decision Chain

The board's approach to the Developer Agreement evolved through a series of decisions from February through April 2026. Each decision responded to new information or shifting board consensus.

#002 February 20, 2026 Active
Verbal Extension Granted

Primary Board met with developer and his attorney. Granted a verbal extension to June 30, 2026. No written agreement was executed. Developer committed to completing outstanding obligations: replace plywood with window near rear awning, remove external gas pipe, identify HVAC unit ownership on roof.

Outcome: Extension granted on good faith. No documentation, no financial security.
▼
#003 March 19, 2026 Active
Extension Reversed — Demand Letter Directed

Joint Primary/Secondary board meeting. Board consensus to reject the February 20 extension and direct attorney Gary to draft a demand letter with an April 22 deadline (30 days). If no movement by April 22, no further extension.

Board members concluded that nothing had moved forward since the February meeting despite developer commitments.

Positions: Wally, Ray, Mark against extension. John Gopplerud in favor. Miles Tarter asked clarifying questions. Gary confirmed all actions within board's legal rights.
Outcome: Demand letter directed but never sent with bond language. Superseded by later decisions.
▼
#004 March 19, 2026 Superseded
$250K Performance Bond Requirement

Board consensus (not a formal vote) that any extension required a $250,000 performance bond. Strategy: start at $250K, expect to negotiate to $150K. This was the third attempt at requiring a bond (see Bond History).

Ray was the hardest pusher for financial security.
Outcome: Never materialized. Primary Board subsequently voted NO bond.
▼
#005 March 19 – April 9, 2026 Active
Bond Requirement Voted Down

Primary Board held a formal vote on the bond requirement and voted NO bond. The verbal extension was granted without financial security of any kind.

Note: The exact date and vote breakdown of this decision have not been recorded. The outcome is confirmed by subsequent board correspondence.

Outcome: Removed the last enforcement mechanism from the extension.
▼
#006 April 8, 2026 Active
June 30 Extension — Final Terms

Primary Board granted a verbal extension to June 30, 2026 with six specific milestones (see Current Milestones). No bond, no intermediate deadlines, no penalties.

Governance note: Jeff and Ray are Residence Board trustees — informed of this decision but non-voting on extension matters. Primary Board holds all extension authority.

Outcome: Current state. All contractual extensions exhausted. Extension is ad hoc with no contractual basis.
