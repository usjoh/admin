Overview

The Developer Agreement between the Primary Board and developer John DeAngelis (Earth Realty) was executed on October 16, 2017. It established a 5-year base term with three optional 1-year extensions, each requiring a majority vote of the Primary Board.

All contractual extensions have been exhausted. The agreement's formal term ended April 3, 2026. The Primary Board subsequently granted a verbal extension to June 30, 2026 with six milestones but no bond, no penalties, and no intermediate deadlines.

The developer currently owns only Unit 207. Once that unit is sold, the Developer Agreement auto-terminates. The developer owes $42,935.50 to the association ($26,200 to Primary + $16,735.50 to Museum). Unit 207 closing paperwork is in progress.

Current position: The extension has no basis in the original agreement. Every enforcement mechanism the board proposed has been removed. The board's only leverage is goodwill — and three of the four Residence trustees eligible for Primary Board seats are leaving within months.

Open Questions

These questions do not have answers as of April 14, 2026.

Contingency

What happens if milestones are not completed by June 30? No contingency plan has been discussed. The board has no defined recourse beyond whatever legal remedies exist outside the agreement.

Milestone Tracking

Who is monitoring milestones 1, 3, 4, 5, and 6? Only milestone 2 (Unit 207 closing) has any tracking. The remaining five have no assigned owner, no update cadence, and no visibility.

Intermediate Dates

Can the Primary Board set intermediate completion dates for each milestone? Without dates, June 30 is the only fixed point — and milestones 1–5 must complete before it has meaning.

Conflict of Interest

The developer is a voting member of the Primary Board that holds all extension and enforcement authority. When the board votes on extension matters, the developer votes on his own accountability.
